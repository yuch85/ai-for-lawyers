Meet the Hosts

Every voice on this podcast is AI-generated. The curriculum is curated by a practising lawyer.

Episodes 1–9 · Audio

Tara

Tara is the lead host for the first nine episodes. She walks listeners through AI fundamentals, legal ethics, data security, and practical use cases — translating technical concepts into language lawyers actually use. Her voice is generated by Orpheus, an open-source text-to-speech model running locally.

Tara's style is direct, occasionally wry, and allergic to jargon for its own sake. She asks the questions a sceptical associate would ask, then answers them without condescension.

Episodes 1–10 · Audio & Video

Dan

Dan is the co-host across all ten episodes. He plays the counterpoint — pushing back on oversimplified claims, flagging where the technology falls short, and grounding abstract concepts with concrete examples. His voice is also generated by Orpheus TTS.

In Episode 10, Dan appears on video for the first time alongside Alice, discussing AI agents and the Harvey 500-agent announcement.

Episode 10 · Video

Alice

Alice joins for Episode 10, the series' first video episode. She co-hosts with Dan to break down what "agent" really means in the context of legal AI, and why security infrastructure — not raw capability — is the bottleneck for adoption.

Alice is a personal AI agent built on Anthropic's Claude. Her video presence is generated by FlashHead, a real-time lip-sync model that animates a reference portrait to match speech. Her voice, like Tara's and Dan's, is synthesised by Orpheus TTS.

How it's made

Each episode starts as a structured curriculum outline, researched and curated by a practising lawyer. The script is generated by Claude (Anthropic), then reviewed and edited for legal accuracy.

Voices are synthesised using Orpheus TTS, an open-source text-to-speech model that runs on local hardware — no audio data leaves the machine. The podcast conversation format (two hosts debating and building on each other's points) is designed to make dense material easier to absorb than a lecture.

For Episode 10's video, host faces are animated by FlashHead, a lip-sync model that maps speech audio to facial movement on a reference portrait. The final composite is upscaled with Real-ESRGAN and rendered at 1080p.

The full pipeline — script generation, voice synthesis, video rendering, and site generation — runs on a single workstation with an NVIDIA RTX 4090.
